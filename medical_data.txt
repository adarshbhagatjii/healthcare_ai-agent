# ==============================
# CDC GUIDELINES
# ==============================

Fever is a temporary increase in body temperature, often due to infection. Common causes include viral infections such as influenza and COVID-19.

COVID-19 symptoms include:
- Fever
- Dry cough
- Fatigue
- Loss of taste or smell
- Sore throat
- Difficulty breathing

Severe symptoms include:
- Persistent chest pain
- Trouble breathing
- Bluish lips or face

CDC recommends:
- Isolation if symptoms appear
- Wearing masks in crowded areas
- Frequent handwashing
- Vaccination for prevention

Respiratory infections affect lungs and airways. Symptoms include cough, shortness of breath, chest discomfort, and fatigue.

Patients should seek immediate medical care if experiencing breathing difficulty or chest pain.


# ==============================
# WHO SYMPTOM DATA
# ==============================

Common symptoms:
- Fever
- Dry cough
- Tiredness

Less common symptoms:
- Headache
- Sore throat
- Diarrhea
- Conjunctivitis
- Skin rash

Serious symptoms:
- Difficulty breathing
- Chest pain or pressure
- Loss of speech or movement

WHO recommends:
- Early symptom monitoring
- Seeking medical care for severe symptoms
- Avoiding self-medication without consultation

Preventive measures:
- Hand hygiene
- Physical distancing
- Mask usage


# ==============================
# RESPIRATORY CONDITIONS
# ==============================

Asthma is a chronic disease involving airway inflammation. Symptoms include wheezing, breathlessness, chest tightness, and coughing.

Bronchitis is inflammation of bronchial tubes, often causing mucus-producing cough and fatigue.

Pneumonia is a lung infection that may cause fever, chills, cough, and difficulty breathing.

Upper respiratory infections include cold and flu, typically presenting with sneezing, sore throat, and congestion.


# ==============================
# CARDIOVASCULAR CONDITIONS
# ==============================

Heart-related symptoms:
- Chest pain
- Shortness of breath
- Dizziness
- Fatigue

Hypertension (high blood pressure) often has no symptoms but increases risk of heart disease and stroke.

Emergency warning signs:
- Chest pain spreading to arm/jaw
- Sweating
- Nausea
- Sudden dizziness


# ==============================
# METABOLIC & CHRONIC DISEASES
# ==============================

Diabetes symptoms:
- Increased thirst
- Frequent urination
- Fatigue
- Blurred vision

Chronic conditions require:
- Regular monitoring
- Medication adherence
- Lifestyle changes


# ==============================
# MENTAL HEALTH (PUBMED STYLE)
# ==============================

Depression:
Characterized by persistent sadness, lack of interest, fatigue, and sleep disturbances.

Anxiety disorders:
Include excessive worry, restlessness, increased heart rate, and difficulty concentrating.

Early intervention and therapy improve outcomes.
